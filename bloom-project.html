Empowering pregnant women in Africa through education, tracking, and community support

Project Overview

Bloom is a comprehensive maternal health platform designed specifically for pregnant women in Nigeria and across Africa. The platform combines daily health education, pregnancy tracking, appointment management, and a unique token-based rewards system to encourage healthy behaviors and regular healthcare engagement.

In a region where maternal mortality rates remain critically high and access to quality healthcare information is limited, Bloom serves as a digital companion throughout pregnancy, providing reliable information, tracking tools, and motivation to help expectant mothers make informed decisions about their health.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: User Engagement & Retention

How do we keep users engaged throughout their 9-month pregnancy journey?

Solution: Implemented a gamified token rewards system that incentivizes healthy behaviors and regular app usage. Users earn tokens for tracking health metrics, attending appointments, and completing educational content, which can be redeemed for tangible benefits like baby products or healthcare services.

Challenge: Data Visualization for Non-Technical Users

How do we present complex health data in an accessible, understandable way?

Solution: Designed intuitive visual charts and graphs with color-coding and simple explanations. Used progressive disclosure to show basic information upfront with the option to dive deeper. Included contextual help and tooltips throughout the interface.

Challenge: Cultural Sensitivity & Localization

How do we ensure content is culturally appropriate and relevant for African women?

Solution: Conducted extensive research with local healthcare providers and pregnant women to understand cultural contexts, preferences, and sensitivities. Worked with medical professionals to ensure all content is medically accurate while being culturally appropriate.

What I Learned

üéØ User-Centered Design is Critical

Working on a healthcare product taught me the importance of deeply understanding user needs. Early user research and testing saved us from building features that looked good but didn't solve real problems. Every design decision needed to be backed by user insights.

‚öñÔ∏è Balancing Complexity and Simplicity

Healthcare involves complex data, but users need simple interfaces. I learned to use progressive disclosure, visual hierarchies, and clear information architecture to make complex information accessible without oversimplifying medical accuracy.

üîÑ Iterative Development Works

Starting with an MVP and iterating based on feedback proved invaluable. We initially over-designed features that users didn't need. Testing early and often helped us focus on what truly mattered and avoid wasted effort.

‚ôø Accessibility is Non-Negotiable

Designing for healthcare means designing for everyone, including users with varying levels of technical literacy, visual impairments, or limited internet connectivity. I learned to implement accessibility best practices from the start, not as an afterthought.

üé® Design Systems Save Time

Creating a component library and design system early on made development faster and ensured consistency across the platform. Reusable components reduced code duplication and made the codebase more maintainable.

üåç Context Matters in Design

What works in Western healthcare apps doesn't always translate to African contexts. I learned to research local healthcare systems, cultural beliefs, and user behaviors to create truly localized solutions rather than just translating interfaces.
